Pizza Recipe

Pizza is one of the world's most popular foods, traditionally consisting of a flattened dough base baked with tomato sauce, cheese, and various toppings. Originating in Naples, Italy, it has evolved into thousands of regional variations globally, ranging from deep-dish to ultra-thin crusts.

Ingredients

  • Dough: 1 store-bought or homemade pizza dough ball
  • Sauce: 1/2 cup crushed San Marzano tomatoes mixed with a pinch of salt
  • Cheese: 4 oz fresh mozzarella, torn or sliced
  • Toppings: Fresh basil leaves and a drizzle of extra virgin olive oil

Instructions

  1. Preheat: Crank your oven as high as it goes (ideally 500°F / 260°C) with a baking sheet or pizza stone inside.
  2. Shape: Stretch the dough on a piece of parchment paper into a 12-inch circle, keeping the edges slightly thicker.
  3. Assemble: Spread the tomato sauce thinly across the dough, then scatter the fresh mozzarella evenly on top.
  4. Bake: Transfer the pizza (with the parchment paper) onto the hot baking sheet and bake for 8 to 10 minutes until the crust is golden and the cheese bubbles.
  5. Finish: Remove from the oven, top immediately with fresh basil leaves, and finish with a quick drizzle of olive oil.
